This project will explore the relationship between macroeconomic variables and stock market performance using time series analysis, with a focus on Granger Causality. The goal is to examine whether major economic indicators such as interest rates, inflation, or unemployment contain useful information for predicting stock market returns. Using historical data from sources like the Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) database and market indices such as the S&P 500, the project will first analyze the statistical properties of each time series, including trends, stationarity, and autocorrelation. After preparing the data, a VAR model will be used to capture the dynamic relationships between the variables. Granger causality tests will then be conducted to determine whether past values of macroeconomic indicators improve predictions of stock market movements beyond what past market returns alone can explain. The results will be interpreted in the context of financial markets and economic theory to evaluate whether macroeconomic conditions appear to influence equity market behavior. Overall, this project aims to demonstrate how time series methods can be applied to better understand the connections between economic fundamentals and stock market dynamics.
